  TRUDY'S HEART FOUNDATION

About Trudy's Heart Foundation

How We Started

The foundation had its beginnings in Aboso, Ghana in 1974 as a way for Gertrude to help, and solicit help for three sibling children: Ebenezer, Georgina and Gloria. They had been abandoned by a working father, and left with an unemployed and disabled mother. Gertrude decided to act. With permission from the mother, she brought the children to her own home to care for them.

Some years later, Gertrude was moved by another encounter of five children whose mother had been murdered by the father. The father then proceeded to take his own life, leaving the very young and very vulnerable children to fend for themselves in an already austere environment. Pastor Paul Mukey, a resident of a nearby village, stepped in to assist, along with Gertrude's help. Gertrude soon came to realize the trend of numerous children with similar disturbing stories in the city of Nkroful and surrounding areas.

Gertrude was moved to do more and felt compelled to adopt, care for and educate a number of these children, and eventually solicited her home to be used as a "Children's Home" in early 2006. Trudy's Heart Foundation has been in operation since then. It is now supported by individuals from all over the world. Major contributions have been made by generous people from the US, Germany, Norway, and Italy. Trudy's Heart Foundation is now home to many orphaned children and struggling families, and has become a source of hope for the hopeless. The organization provides services such as the Children's Home, assisting widows in hardship circumstances with essential items and services, assisting other charities by supplying them with recieved donations, and facilitating vocational training for the youth through the Nkroful Youth Association (formed in 2014 to help provide jobs for unemployed young men and women).


About The Founder

Gertrude Heman-Ackah


Gertrude is a native of Ghana. She was born and raised in Aboso, Ghana. She has two adult children: Henry Yawson and Ekow Yawson, both of whom reside in the United States of America. She also has an adopted son: George Mensah. With a soft heart for children and a love for people, she has touched the lives of numerous people. Within the past ten years, Gertrude has helped to improve the lives of so many.

Gertrude worked as a middle school and high school Home Economics and English teacher in Ghana and Nigeria from 1972 to 1984. She moved to the United States of America in 1986, where she trained and certified as a Phlebotomist in the states of Maryland, Virginia and the District of Columbia. She returned to Takoradi, Ghana in 2002 with her late husband, Dr. Samuel Heman-Ackah. She has since been helping small groups and individuals through her youth organizations and charities.
